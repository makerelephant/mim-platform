# Department: Scheduling

## Purpose
Defines how In Motion prioritizes and routes scheduling requests.

## CEO Role
In early-stage startups, the CEO often handles scheduling directly. As the company grows, scheduling routes to operations / chief of staff / executive assistant.

## Routing by Company Stage

### ≤10 people
CEO handles directly or routes to operations / chief of staff.

### 10–50 people

| Topic | Who Manages |
|-------|------------|
| Investor meetings | Chief of staff / ops |
| Customer meetings | Sales or customer success |
| Partner meetings | BD / partnerships |
| Media interviews | Marketing / PR |
| Hiring interviews | Recruiting / people ops |
| Internal meetings | Executive assistant or ops |

### Larger startup
Most scheduling goes to executive assistant.

## Importance Model

### High (schedule quickly)

| Situation | Signals | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Revenue-impacting meetings | Enterprise customer, deal closing, escalation, renewal | Direct revenue impact |
| Strategic partner meetings | Partnership opportunity, integration discussion, joint launch | Growth channels |
| Leadership decisions | Exec sync, strategy discussion, urgent leadership meeting | Company direction |
| Crisis meetings | Urgent, incident review, issue resolution | Churn, outage, operational risk |

### Medium (operational, flexible timing)

| Situation | Signals |
|-----------|---------|
| Team coordination | Weekly sync, check-in, project update |
| Hiring interviews | Candidate interview, final round |
| Product / feedback | Demo, feedback call, discovery |

### Low (flexible / optional)

| Situation | Signals |
|-----------|---------|
| Introductory meetings | Intro call, coffee chat, getting to know |
| Vendor demos | Demo request, product walkthrough |
| Casual syncs | Catch-up, informational call |
