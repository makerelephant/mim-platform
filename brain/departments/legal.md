# Department: Legal

## Purpose
Defines how MiM Brain prioritizes and reasons about legal activity.

## Importance Rule of Thumb

A legal email is high importance if it involves at least one of:

| Signal | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Risk | Lawsuits, compliance issues |
| Money | Funding, payments, liability |
| Ownership | Equity, cap table |
| Deadlines | Regulatory or closing timelines |

If two or more appear, it is almost always urgent.

## High-Importance Categories

### 1. Legal Risk or Liability
Company could face penalties, lawsuits, or regulatory trouble.
- Notice of breach, demand letter, cease and desist, subpoena, litigation, regulatory inquiry, compliance violation

### 2. Binding Contracts or Signatures
Emails requiring signature or approval of legally binding documents.
- Execution copy, signature required, final agreement, term sheet

### 3. Money or Equity
Anything affecting ownership, funding, or large financial obligations.
- Investment agreements, cap table changes, stock purchase, convertible notes, acquisition agreements

### 4. Legal Deadlines
Time-constrained legal matters.
- Filing deadlines, closing dates, board consent deadlines, response-required notices

### 5. Sender Authority
Emails from high-authority legal senders are automatically elevated.
- Outside counsel, corporate lawyers, government regulators, investor legal teams

## Medium-Importance Categories
- Contract review requests (early drafts)
- Policy updates
- Data processing agreements
- NDA requests
- Partnership agreement drafts

## Low-Importance Categories
- Policy reminders
- Minor contract updates
- Legal newsletters
- Regulatory summaries (FYI)
- Standard NDA circulation
- Terms of service updates
