# Department: Accounting & Finance

## Purpose
Defines how In Motion prioritizes and routes accounting and finance activity.

## CEO Role
Approval + visibility, not day-to-day handling. Most finance execution routes to a single finance owner in early-stage startups.

## Routing Rules

| Email Type | Forward To |
|------------|-----------|
| Investor financial questions | CFO / finance lead |
| Accounting reports | CFO / finance lead |
| Invoices / vendor payments | Finance / ops |
| Payroll | HR / people ops |
| Taxes | External accountant |
| Budget planning | COO / CFO |
| Expense reimbursements | Finance / ops |
| Banking issues | Finance / ops |

## Importance Model

### High (immediate attention)

| Situation | Signals | Risk |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Payments due or overdue | Invoice overdue, wire request, payment required | Service disruption, penalties, vendor damage |
| Payroll processing | Payroll approval, payroll error | Employee impact, legal problems |
| Banking / fraud alerts | Unusual activity, chargeback, account verification | Fraud, blocked payments |
| Tax deadlines | Filing deadline, IRS notice, audit request | Penalties, legal risk |

### Medium (operational, within a day or two)

| Situation | Signals |
|-----------|---------|
| Accounting updates | Monthly financials, P&L, balance sheet, reconciliation |
| Expense approvals | Reimbursement request, expense report |
| Vendor billing questions | Invoice discrepancy, billing clarification |

### Low (informational)

| Situation | Signals |
|-----------|---------|
| Receipts / confirmations | Payment receipt, transaction summary |
| System notifications | Automated reports, dashboard updates |
| Vendor marketing | Product updates, newsletters, feature announcements |
