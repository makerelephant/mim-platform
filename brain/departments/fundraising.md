# Department: Fundraising

## Purpose
Defines how MiM Brain prioritizes, scores, and reasons about fundraising activity.

## Impact Scoring Model

Scale: 0–100 impact score — estimated influence on probability of closing a round.

### Critical Impact (85–100)

| Factor | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Traction / Metrics | 95 | Strongest driver. Real growth reduces investor risk. |
| Market Size (TAM) | 85 | Investors need venture-scale outcomes. |
| Lead Investor Commitment | 85 | Once a lead commits, the round usually fills. |

### High Impact (65–80)

| Factor | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Warm Introductions | 80 | Investors trust curated deal flow far more than cold outreach. |
| Pitch Quality / Story | 75 | Great storytelling increases conviction dramatically. |
| Founder Credibility | 75 | Background, past success, expertise matter heavily. |
| Investor Meetings | 70 | Where deals move forward or die. |
| Fundraising Momentum | 70 | Social proof drives investor urgency. |
| Network Strength | 65 | Determines access to top-tier investors. |

### Medium Impact (40–60)

| Factor | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Market Timing | 60 | Being early/late to a trend impacts appetite. |
| Pitch Deck | 55 | Important but rarely the deciding factor alone. |
| Financial Model | 50 | Mostly for diligence credibility. |
| Investor Targeting | 50 | Prevents wasted effort. |
| Data Room Quality | 45 | Matters during diligence, not for initial interest. |
| Outreach Volume | 45 | Fills pipeline but doesn't guarantee success. |
| Follow-ups | 45 | Persistence matters but can't fix weak fundamentals. |

### Lower Impact (10–35)

| Factor | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| Demo Days | 35 | Mostly marketing exposure. |
| Cold Emails | 30 | Conversion rates are extremely low. |
| Meeting Logistics | 15 | Operational detail. |
| Fancy Pitch Design | 10 | Investors care about substance. |

## The Fundraising Equation

```
Probability of Raising ≈ Traction × Market Size × Founder Credibility × Investor Access × Momentum
```

Everything else (decks, models, outreach tools) supports these core variables.

## Five Things That Actually Close Rounds

1. Traction
2. Large market
3. Strong narrative
4. Warm investor access
5. Momentum once fundraising starts

These five account for ~70–80% of fundraising success.
