# Email Classification Rule: Scheduling

## Category ID
`scheduling`

## Inbound From
- Investors
- Customers (enterprise prospects, key clients)
- Partners (integration, distribution, strategic)
- Press / media (interviews, podcasts)
- Recruiting candidates
- Vendors (demo requests)
- Internal leadership team
- Sales team (customer wants CEO involved)
- Operations / chief of staff
- Board members
- Google Calendar (accepts, declines, updates)

## Outbound To
- Operations / chief of staff
- Executive assistant
- Sales / customer success (customer meetings)
- BD / partnerships (partner meetings)
- Marketing / PR (media)
- Recruiting / people ops (hiring interviews)

## Signals

### Scheduling Language
Schedule, find time, availability, calendar, coordinate, meeting request, book time, quick call, sync, reschedule, postpone, "can we find time", "when are you free", "let's schedule", "are you available"

### Calendar Notifications
Accepted, declined, updated invitation, tentative, new invitation, cancelled, rescheduled

### Routing Rule
If an email asks "when can we talk?" — route to the person who owns the relationship.

| Relationship | Owner |
|-------------|-------|
| Customer | Sales / CS |
| Partner | BD / partnerships |
| Investor | CEO / chief of staff |
| Hiring | Recruiting |
| Media | Marketing / PR |

## Importance

### High
- Revenue-impacting: enterprise customer call, deal closing, customer escalation, renewal discussion
- Strategic partner: partnership opportunity, integration discussion, joint launch planning
- Leadership decisions: exec sync, strategy discussion, urgent leadership meeting
- Crisis: urgent, incident review, customer escalation, issue resolution

### Medium
- Regular team coordination: weekly sync, check-in, project update, team meeting
- Hiring interviews: candidate interview, final interview, recruiting conversation
- Product or feedback: product demo, feedback call, customer discovery

### Low
- Introductory meetings: intro call, quick chat, coffee chat
- Vendor or tool demos: demo request, product walkthrough, sales presentation
- Casual syncs: catch-up, informational call, general discussion
